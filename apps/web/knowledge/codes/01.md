## Common defences
- The restriction sign or yellow line was obscured, faded, or absent on the day.
- The driver was actively loading/unloading from kerbside premises (single yellow exemption — most boroughs allow brief loading).
- The vehicle had broken down at the position.
- The driver held a valid Blue Badge displayed correctly.
- A valid resident or business permit covered the bay at the time.

## Common rebuttals from council
- "Yellow lines are clear in the CEO photograph" — counter with a same-day wider photo showing fade extent or obstruction.
- "Loading was not observed during the CEO observation period" — counter with timestamped delivery-note / shop-receipt evidence showing activity in the gaps.

## Notes
Code 01 is the catch-all London single-yellow code. Loading exemptions
vary borough-by-borough — TfL Red Routes have stricter loading rules
than borough single yellows. Some boroughs have a 20-minute soft cap on
loading activity even where the sign permits it.
