## Common defences
- The kerb blips indicating no-loading were absent, faded, or contradicted the upright sign.
- Loading was permitted by the upright sign at the time of contravention.
- The driver was momentarily setting down or taking up a passenger (not parking).
- Brief stop during which the vehicle was constantly attended.

## Common rebuttals from council
- "Kerb markings clearly visible" — counter with a wider photo.
- "Vehicle stationary for X minutes therefore parked" — counter with the legal definition of 'parked' (not the same as stopped).

## Notes
Code 02 is the double-yellow / no-loading version of code 01. The
no-loading kerb blips are the critical signing element — if they're
faded or missing, the substantive restriction is unsigned and the
upright restriction sign alone is not sufficient.
