## Common defences
- A valid resident / business / visitor permit was displayed but the CEO did not see it (sun-strip glare, slipped on dashboard).
- A virtual permit was registered against the VRM in the borough's permit system.
- A Blue Badge was displayed (entitles parking in residents' bays in most boroughs).
- The bay's controlling sign was obscured or markings faded.

## Common rebuttals from council
- "Permit not visible in CEO photographs" — counter with a same-day photo of the dashboard.
- "Bay clearly marked as residents only" — counter with a wide-angle showing fade extent of bay markings or the small/distant sign.

## Notes
Code 12 is among the most-appealed codes in London. Virtual permit
systems are now common in Westminster, Camden, Lambeth, Hackney, and
Islington — if the user holds a virtual permit, ask the council to
check the live permit database against the VRM at the time of the
PCN. A discrepancy between live permit data and the CEO's record is
strong grounds for cancellation.
