## Common defences
- The suspension notice was not displayed at the bay when the vehicle was parked.
- The suspension notice was affixed after the vehicle was already parked (the council's general practice is 7 days' notice; absent that, the suspension is procedurally defective).
- The dates on the suspension notice did not include the date of the alleged contravention.
- The council's published list of bay suspensions did not show this bay as suspended.

## Common rebuttals from council
- "Suspension notice was in place" — counter with a same-day photo of the bay showing no notice.
- "Notice was visible from the carriageway" — counter with photos taken from the driver's seat.

## Notes
Code 21 cancellations are common when the appellant can show the
suspension notice was late or absent. The council's record of when
the notice was affixed (often recorded in the suspension job log) is
disclosable; ask for it formally if disputing.
