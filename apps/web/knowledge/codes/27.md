## Common defences
- The dropped kerb was not a footway crossing for vehicle access — it was a pedestrian dropped kerb only (vehicle access prohibition does not apply).
- The driver was actively loading at the address served by the dropped kerb, with the householder's consent.
- The crossing was not in lawful use (sealed off, no associated driveway).

## Common rebuttals from council
- "Dropped kerb is for vehicle access" — counter with photos of the kerb showing it is solely a pedestrian crossing without associated driveway.

## Notes
Section 86 of the TMA 2004 prohibits parking adjacent to a dropped
footway that has been lowered for vehicle access — pure pedestrian
dropped kerbs (eg. at a bus stop or for accessibility) are NOT
covered. The distinction is decisive.
