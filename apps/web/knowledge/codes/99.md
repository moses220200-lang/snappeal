## Common defences
- The vehicle was halted by traffic conditions, not voluntarily stopped (regulation 18(3) exception).
- The vehicle stopped to give precedence to a pedestrian (this is required, not an offence).
- Brief stop in response to an emergency / police direction.

## Common rebuttals from council
- "Stopped within the zig-zag area for X seconds" — counter with the regulation 18 exceptions (giving precedence, traffic-stopped).

## Notes
Code 99 has the highest acquittal rate at the London tribunal among
moving traffic codes — the regulation 18 exceptions are rarely well
applied by CEOs.
