A commercial driver delivering chilled goods to a restaurant on a TfL Red Route (Marylebone Road) received a PCN under code 27 (parked on a Red Route in a designated bay during prescribed hours). The bay was a Red Route loading bay marked with the standard 'Loading Only' upright sign with no kerbside markings prohibiting loading at the time of arrival.

The CEO's notes recorded a twelve-minute observation period during which the vehicle was stationary; the CEO did not observe any movement of goods between the vehicle and the premises. The PCN was issued at the close of the observation.

The driver's informal representation included six timestamped photographs from his own phone, taken at one-to-two-minute intervals over a fourteen-minute window straddling the observation: photo 1 showed him in the rear of the vehicle on the cargo lift; photo 2 showed him at the restaurant's rear delivery door with a crate; photo 3 showed him returning to the vehicle empty-handed; photo 4 showed him pulling a fresh crate; and so on. The metadata on the photographs confirmed the timestamps.

The driver also attached the delivery note signed by the restaurant manager confirming acceptance of seventeen separate items between 14:02 and 14:18, and a one-line statement from the manager confirming the driver had been actively moving stock the entire time.

TfL cancelled the PCN at the informal representation stage. The decision letter referred to "continuous loading activity demonstrated by the photographic evidence."

What did the work: the photographic series defeated the CEO's static "no movement observed" record by showing movement happening in the gaps between the CEO's own observation moments. The independent corroboration from the restaurant manager removed any need for the council to take the driver's word for it. The driver's notes never used the phrase "I was loading" alone — every claim was tied to a specific timestamped image or signature.
