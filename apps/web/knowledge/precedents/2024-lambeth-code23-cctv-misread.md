The appellant received a CCTV-issued PCN by post (code 23 — parked in a parking place or area not designated for that class of vehicle) referring to a VRM ending in '8WZ'. The appellant's vehicle ended in 'BWZ' — a one-character difference in the third character.

Lambeth's standard PCN photograph was a single ANPR-cropped image of the rear plate taken at low light. The character difference was visually plausible (an italicised '8' in the council's typeface, photographed against a smudged plate, can read as 'B'). The remaining characters and the make/model in the appellant's V5C matched the council's record.

The appellant's informal representation attached: a current photograph of the rear plate taken in daylight, clearly showing 'BWZ'; a photograph of the V5C registration document showing the correct VRM; and a one-line statement that the appellant had not been at the location on the date in question.

Lambeth's cancellation letter accepted that "on the balance of the photographic evidence the VRM as captured does not match the vehicle to which this notice was addressed." The PCN was cancelled in full and the council confirmed that no further action would be taken in respect of the vehicle.

The appellant requested, and received, written confirmation that the council's records had been updated so the PCN would not appear on a future credit check or DVLA fitness check.

What did the work: the side-by-side daylight photograph removed any ambiguity about the actual plate's content. The pre-emptive request for written confirmation closed off the secondary risk of the cancelled PCN lingering on the council's records. The appellant did not assert what had happened on the date — only that it was not their vehicle — which kept the representation tight and avoided opening unnecessary surfaces.
