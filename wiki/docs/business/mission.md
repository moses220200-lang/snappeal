# Mission

> **To make appealing a London parking ticket as easy as paying it.**

A Penalty Charge Notice is supposed to be an instrument of fair traffic enforcement. In practice, paying is easy and contesting is hard. The discount window is short, the language is bureaucratic, the contravention codes are opaque, the grounds for appeal are buried in the Traffic Management Act 2004, and every borough has its own portal and rules.

Most people pay even when they shouldn't, because the path of least resistance is to pay.

Snappeal exists to flip that asymmetry. We give the motorist the same five-tap experience the council gives them for payment, only for the other answer: *I'm appealing this.*

Concretely, the mission is met when:

- A Londoner can submit a competent appeal from their phone in **five taps**, from the moment they walk back to the car.
- The appeal cites the **right statutory ground** and the **right contravention code** without the user having to know those things exist.
- The cost is **a fraction of the PCN** — never enough to make appealing feel like an extra punishment.
- The act is **honest**. Snappeal drafts arguments grounded in the user's actual facts. It will never invent evidence, exaggerate circumstances, or impersonate a solicitor.

We measure ourselves against one number: **what percentage of Londoners who feel their PCN is unfair actually appeal it?** Today that figure is roughly half a percent. We want it to be closer to half.
