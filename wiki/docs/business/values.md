# Values

Last refreshed **2026-05-27 (v0.3.10)**.

How we work, in five sentences.

### 1. Plain English over legal English
Every screen, every letter, every doc on this wiki must be readable by someone who has never heard of "the Traffic Management Act 2004". If a sentence requires legal vocabulary, we rewrite it or explain the term inline.

### 2. Honest evidence, always
We help the user articulate what actually happened. We do not invent mitigating circumstances, exaggerate signage problems, or pretend the user was a blue-badge holder when they weren't. A weak honest appeal is better than a strong dishonest one — for the user, for us, and for the system.

### 3. Pay once, get the whole thing
£2.99 buys an automated appeal — the AI-drafted letter and (from v0.2) its submission to the council. One-off, non-refundable: you pay for the work, we deliver the work. No subscriptions, no "premium" tier that adds the bits the appeal actually needed, no upsells. If our system fails to deliver, we refund — that's a service-quality remedy, not an outcome guarantee.

### 4. Speed beats sophistication
Five taps to a draft letter. Every screen has at most one decision. If a feature can't survive contact with a stressed driver on a phone in the rain, it doesn't ship.

### 5. The council is the customer too
A council reviewer reading our letter has thirty seconds. We respect that. Our letters are short, cite the right code, attach the right evidence, and don't pad. If a council reviewer dreads opening an "ParkingRabbit-generated" letter, we have already lost.
