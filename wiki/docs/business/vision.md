# Vision

> **In five years, no Londoner pays a parking ticket they don't deserve to pay.**

Today, around **9.5 million PCNs** are issued across London every year[^1]. Only about **0.45%** are challenged at the London Tribunals stage[^1]. The councils know this. Their enforcement models are calibrated to it.

Our five-year vision is to change those numbers.

**Where we want to be by 2031:**

1. **One in three** London PCN recipients opens Snappeal within 14 days of receiving their ticket — because their council's "Pay" page now sits next to a friend's "Appeal it" recommendation.
2. **One in ten** PCNs in London is contested via Snappeal.
3. **More than half** of those contested PCNs are cancelled at the informal-representations stage, without ever reaching the tribunal — saving councils the administrative cost and saving motorists the stress.
4. Snappeal becomes the **fastest, most honest** way to push back on any civil-enforcement notice — extending from PCNs to bus lane PCNs, moving traffic PCNs, ULEZ, congestion charge, and ultimately to private parking operators governed by the new POPLA-replacement regime.

**What this is not.** We are not trying to flood the system with weak appeals. The vision succeeds only if the appeals we draft are *better* than what the median motorist would write themselves — clearer, more grounded in evidence, and easier for the council reviewer to process. If we degrade quality to win volume, the councils will respond by making appeal procedures harder for everyone, and the system gets worse.

So our north star isn't appeals submitted. It's **the number of unfair PCNs that get cancelled because of us**.

[^1]: London Councils, *Enforcement and appeals statistics 2024-25*. <https://www.londoncouncils.gov.uk/news-and-press-releases/2025/london-councils-enforcement-and-appeals-statistics-2024-25>
