# How to appeal a London parking ticket

The short version: **most PCNs that get appealed get cancelled at the first or second stage**, before any tribunal involvement. The first thing to do is *not* pay — at least not immediately. Once you pay, the appeal route is closed.

## Before you do anything

1. **Photograph the PCN.** Both sides if it's a windscreen ticket. Make sure the contravention code, date, time, location and PCN reference number are all readable. Use ParkingRabbit's photo step, or just your camera roll.

2. **Photograph the scene.** While you're still by the car: photograph the signs, any bay markings, the surrounding street, the position of the car. If there's a suspension notice, photograph the dates clearly. If something is obscured by a vehicle, a tree, scaffolding — photograph that too.

3. **Note the time** you took the photos. Modern phones embed this automatically; don't disable timestamps.

4. **Do not move the car if it's safe to leave.** Photographs are stronger when the car is in the position the PCN was issued for.

## Decide if you have grounds

Be honest with yourself. Pay if:

- You parked on a double yellow when you knew the restriction applied.
- The signs were clearly visible and you missed them.
- You overstayed on pay-and-display by more than a few minutes.
- You parked in a residents' bay without a permit and you knew it.

ParkingRabbit if any of the below are true:

- **The signs were unclear, obscured, contradictory, or absent.** (Code 01, 12, 16, 19, 21, 24, 30.)
- **You were actively loading or unloading goods.** With continuous activity, not just the engine running. (Code 02, 25.)
- **A valid permit, pay-and-display ticket, or paid mobile session covered the vehicle.** (Code 12, 16, 19, 30.)
- **You displayed a valid Blue Badge** with the clock set correctly. (Code 40.)
- **There was a medical or other emergency** with documentary evidence. (Code 47, 99.)
- **The vehicle had genuinely broken down** and you arranged recovery.
- **You weren't the registered keeper** at the relevant time.
- **The contravention as described didn't actually occur** — the bay wasn't suspended, the restriction wasn't in force, the warden recorded the wrong vehicle.

If any of those apply, you have a real case.

## The four stages

The full mechanism is documented in [legal/representations-and-appeals.md](../legal/representations-and-appeals.md). The short version:

1. **Informal representation** (within 14 days, while the 50% discount window is open). Decided by a council officer. Most appeals win or lose here.

2. **Formal representation** (within 28 days of the Notice to Owner). Decided by a council adjudication team applying the [six TMA 2004 statutory grounds](../legal/tma-2004.md).

3. **London Tribunals appeal** (within 28 days of a Notice of Rejection). Decided by an independent adjudicator. In 2024-25, 49.4% of these appeals were won by the motorist.

4. **Statutory declaration** at the Traffic Enforcement Centre. Only relevant if you never received the prior notices.

## The strength score

Every draft comes back with a **0–100 appeal-strength score** rendered as a green / amber / red badge above the Pay button. The score reflects the **evidence you've supplied**, not the abstract merit of the legal argument:

- **80–100 (green) — Strong appeal.** Your evidence supports the chosen ground clearly. Pay button reads "Submit for £2.99".
- **50–79 (amber) — Solid appeal.** The case is reasonable but not airtight.
- **Below 50 (red) — Weak appeal warning.** A red panel appears above the Pay button with a one-sentence rationale and up to 3 specific evidence asks ("Add a photo of the suspension sign", "Describe what you saw when you returned"). The Pay button rebrands to "Submit anyway for £2.99" — you can still proceed, but eyes open.

If you didn't attach any photos and your notes are under 50 characters, the score is automatically capped at 45 — you can lift it instantly by adding a photo or a sentence of detail.

## How long it takes

Wall-clock from PCN issue to final outcome:

- **Won at stage 1**: 2–4 weeks total.
- **Won at stage 2**: 8–12 weeks total.
- **Won at stage 3**: 16–24 weeks total.

You won't be chased for payment while a representation is live, and the discount window is paused during stage 1.
